10.1.1 Temperature Measurement Bridge
The temperature sensor is a negative temperature coefficient (NTC) thermistor. As it gets
hot, the resistance decreases linearly with temperature, in contrast to a typical resistive
element that sees an increase in resistance with temperature.
If we build a voltage divider with the thermistor and another fixed resistor, we can use the
output voltage to calculate the thermistor resistance, thereby determining the temperature.
However, as room temperature is not perfectly constant, we need to have our quiescent
operating point be adjustable.
